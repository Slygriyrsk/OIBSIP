Biography

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer known for his exceptional batting skills and leadership on the cricket field. He was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India.

In 2013, Virat Kohli was appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team in ODIs, and he later took over as the captain in Test cricket as well.

Under his leadership, the Indian cricket team achieved numerous successes, including historic Test series wins in Australia.

Achievements

  1. 2008 - Made his international debut for the Indian cricket team in August 2008.
  2. 2010 - Scored his first international century in an ODI against Sri Lanka. Established himself as a key player in the Indian batting lineup.
  3. 2011 - Played a crucial role in India's victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup. Emerging Player of the Tournament in the ICC Cricket World Cup. Achieved the No. 1 ranking in ICC ODI batting rankings for the first time.
  4. 2012 - Became the fastest player to score 1,000 runs in ODIs, achieving this milestone in just 24 innings. Made his Test captaincy debut against Australia in December.
  5. 2013 - Scored four centuries in four consecutive Test matches against Australia. Became the fastest player to score 5,000 runs in ODIs. Received the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year.
  6. 2014 - Set a new record for the most centuries in a single IPL season (4). Scored over 2,000 runs in international cricket during the year.
  7. 2015 - Led the Indian cricket team in the ICC Cricket World Cup. Became the fastest player to score 7,000 runs in ODIs. Received the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year for the second consecutive year.
  8. 2016 - Scored three double centuries in Test matches in a calendar year, a record for an Indian batsman. Became the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats.
  9. 2017 - Led India to a historic Test series win against Australia in Australia. Scored over 2,800 international runs during the year. Received the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year for the third consecutive year.
  10. 2018 - Became the fastest player to score 8,000 and 9,000 runs in ODIs. Led India to a Test series win against South Africa in South Africa. Received the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year for the fourth consecutive year.
  11. 2019 - Led India to the semi-finals of the ICC Cricket World Cup. Became the first cricketer to score 20,000 international runs in a single decade. Scored his 70th international century.
  12. 2020 - Announced stepping down as the captain of the Indian T20I team in December 2020.
